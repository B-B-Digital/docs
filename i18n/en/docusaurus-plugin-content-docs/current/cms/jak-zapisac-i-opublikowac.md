# How do I save and publish changes?

The panel saves your work continuously, so you don't need to worry about losing text you've typed. That doesn't mean, though, that every keystroke immediately lands on the site your clients see. There's an important difference between "saved" and "published," and it's worth understanding from day one.

## The draft

Whenever you edit any document — adding a sentence to a service description, fixing a typo in a bio, updating a phone number — the change is saved right away as a **draft**. Only you see it, once you're logged in to the panel. You can close the tab halfway through an edit, come back an hour or a week later, and your changes will be waiting exactly where you left them.

Next to the title of a document with unpublished changes, you'll see a small dot. That's the signal that something is waiting to go live.

## Publishing

Only clicking the **Publish** button in the top-right corner moves the draft onto the live site. From that moment, everyone sees it — the patient looking for a dentist in your town, the client checking a law firm's offering, the workshop attendee wanting to know more about you as a coach.

Until you click Publish, nothing changes on the outside. You can spend several days editing a new hire's bio, refining sentences, coming back to the same document again and again — and only once you're happy with it, make it public with one click.

## What if you forget to publish

It happens: someone makes a change, closes the panel, and forgets that last step — then asks us why the site still shows the old information. This is the most common cause of that particular question. So if something on the site doesn't match what you expect, that button is the first thing worth checking.
