# How do I add a customer testimonial

This guide only matters if your site has a customer testimonials section — not every project includes one.

For a dental practice, a law firm, or a coaching business, testimonials are often the strongest argument a site can make — stronger than any description of your own services, because it comes from someone who's actually used them.

## Author and testimonial text

For each testimonial, you fill in the author's name and, optionally, their role or context — "Patient since 2020", "Corporate client", "Mentoring program participant". The testimonial text itself is plain text, no formatting — genuine, specific sentences work better than generic praise.

## Rating and order

Each testimonial gets a rating from 1 to 5 stars, plus a number that sets its display order — exactly like with services or team members: the lower the number, the higher it appears on the list.

## How many testimonials to add

We recommend **3 testimonials** as a full set — that's usually what fits in a single row of cards. If you have more, the upper limit is 6 (two full rows of three). Try to avoid odd numbers above three, like five testimonials — the last row of cards ends up looking uneven, with one card left stranded on its own.

## What you won't find here

If your site also shows an aggregate rating from Google or Trustpilot next to your testimonials (something like "4.9 based on 120 reviews"), that **is not edited in this list**. That aggregate rating comes from a separate setting we configure for you on our end — don't go looking for it among the individual customer testimonials in the panel.

That's the last of the guides on editing individual content types in the CMS. If you don't have three testimonials ready yet, start with whatever you already have on hand — you can always add more later.
