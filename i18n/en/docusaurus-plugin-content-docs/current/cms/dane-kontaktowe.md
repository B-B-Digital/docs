# How do I update my contact details

Your address, phone number, and opening hours are the details visitors reach for first — often before reading anything else on the site. It's worth keeping them current, especially since this is one of those sections that's easy to forget when things change seasonally.

## Address, phone, email

These three fields are plain text — enter them exactly as you want them to appear on the site, for example on a contact card or in the footer. The address field can span several lines if your location needs it — unit number, floor, extra directions.

## Precise map location

If your site has a map, it relies on the address text by default. You can also set precise coordinates (latitude and longitude) so the pin on the map points exactly at your door rather than the middle of the street — this field is optional, and it's easiest to set up together with us if precision matters to you.

## Opening hours

Opening hours are a list of rows — each row is a range of days paired with hours, for example "Mon–Fri" and "9:00–17:00", or "Sat" and "Closed". You can add, remove, and reorder these rows freely, so if you only open on Saturdays once a month, you can describe that in one row instead of hunting for an extra field.

This is a good section to revisit around holidays and long weekends — the May bank holidays, All Saints' Day, the closure around the New Year. Nothing frustrates a client more than showing up to locked doors while the site still says "open." After any change, **remember to save and publish** — otherwise the new hours stay stuck in a draft ([how do I save and publish](./jak-zapisac-i-opublikowac.md)).
