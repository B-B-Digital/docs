# How do I fill in the SEO tab?

Open almost any document in the panel and you'll notice two tabs at the top of the form: Content and SEO. The first one is the text and photos visible on the page. The second one governs how that page shows up in Google search results — and even though none of your clients will ever see it directly, it's often what decides whether they find you at all.

## What's in this tab

The fields in the SEO tab repeat across nearly every document type:

- Page title — the text shown as the clickable heading of the Google result, and on the browser tab
- Meta description — a short snippet under the title, meant to encourage a click
- Social share image — the image shown when someone pastes a link to your site into Facebook or a message
- Hide from search engines — an option you'll rarely need, for example for a page that isn't live yet

A good title and description don't need to read like a list of keywords. "Nowak & Partners Law Firm — Family Law in Poznań" tells both the search engine and the client more than a bare "law firm family law poznan cheap fast" ever could.

## The exception: the Services Page

One document in the panel looks different from the rest. The **Services Page** has only an SEO tab, with no Content tab at all — and that's not a bug or a missing feature. The list of services shown on that page is built automatically from the individual Service entries. Since all of the page's content comes from there, there's nothing left to fill in besides SEO. If you want to change the order or content of the services shown, go to the [Service](./usluga.md) document instead — not the Services Page.

## Do I have to fill this in everywhere

In practice, it's worth spending a moment on this for the pages you actually care about ranking in Google — the homepage, the services page, blog posts. An empty SEO field won't break the page, but a well-filled one can be the difference between showing up in search results and staying invisible.
