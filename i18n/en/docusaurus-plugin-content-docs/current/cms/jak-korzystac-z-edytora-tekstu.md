# How do I use the text editor?

Short fields, like a service title or a phone number, are just plain typing. Longer stretches of text — the introduction on the About page, a service's full description, a team member's biography, a blog post's body — open in a different, more capable editor that works much like Word or Google Docs.

## Getting the toolbar to appear

Select a piece of text with your mouse or keyboard, and a small **toolbar** with icons appears above the selection. It isn't shown all the time, only when something is selected — that's deliberate, so the field doesn't look cluttered while you're simply typing.

You'll typically reach for the toolbar to:

- bold the phrase that matters most
- italicize a word, for example a procedure's name in Latin
- add a subheading once the text gets long enough to benefit from being broken into sections
- insert a link, for example to an article you're referencing
- mark a quote when you're citing someone

The exact set of buttons varies slightly from field to field — a service description has second- and third-level headings plus a quote block, while a team bio tends to be simpler. That's intentional: each field is scaled to how long and complex the text in it usually is.

## Paragraphs and line breaks

Enter starts a new paragraph, exactly like in any text editor you're already used to. You don't need to format anything extra — the spacing between paragraphs is handled by the site's template.

## Headings, in moderation

If you're describing a long procedure — say, the stages of a root canal treatment, or the different packages in a legal service — a heading every few paragraphs genuinely helps the reader find their way. A heading every two sentences, on the other hand, stops making sense and makes the text look chopped up rather than organized.
