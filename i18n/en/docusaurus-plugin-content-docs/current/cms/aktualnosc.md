# How do I add a news item

**This guide is only relevant if your site has a news or announcements section** — not every site has one, so if you don't see this option in the panel menu, feel free to skip it.

A news item is a quicker, simpler format than a blog post — good for short announcements like holiday hours, a vacation notice, attending an industry conference, or a new certification. It doesn't call for a full article.

## Title, date, and description

The title and publish date work the same way as they do for blog posts — the date determines the order on the list, with the newest at the top. The description is a short piece of text, usually a few sentences, without paragraph breaks or headings — this isn't a spot for the rich text editor, just a concise piece of information.

## External link

If a news item should point somewhere else — say, an event page, an article in the local press, or a sign-up form — you can add an external link. This field is optional: if the announcement is complete on its own, just leave it blank.

A news item deliberately has no photo and no rich body text like a blog post does — it's the fastest way to announce something briefly, without extra steps.
