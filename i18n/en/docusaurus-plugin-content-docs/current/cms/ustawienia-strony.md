# How do I change my company name, logo, and social media

We're starting a new set of guides — this time going through the panel document by document, in the same order they appear in your left-hand menu. If you're not yet sure how to find your way around the panel, start with [navigating the panel](./jak-poruszac-sie-po-panelu.md) first — this guide assumes you've already been there.

"Site Settings" is different from most of the other sections in that it covers the whole website at once, not a single page. Your company name, logo, browser tab icon, and social media links all show up in multiple places at the same time — in the header, the footer, sometimes in Google search results. Changing something here shows up everywhere as soon as you publish.

This is a **single document for the entire site** — there's no list to browse and no "add new" button, which is a question we get from clients fairly often. You can read more about the difference between this kind of document and a list in [one page or a list of entries](./jedna-strona-czy-lista-wpisow.md).

## Company name and logo

The "Company Name" field is plain text — we use it, among other places, in the browser tab title and anywhere the site refers to you by name. You upload the logo like any other photo (we cover uploading and cropping [here](./jak-wgrywac-i-kadrowac-zdjecia.md)) — it appears in the header and usually in the footer too.

## Favicon, the little icon in the browser tab

The favicon is that tiny icon shown next to your page title on the browser tab — it's what makes your site stand out among ten other open tabs. A simple, square symbol works best: your initials or a simplified version of your logo, without fine detail that would get lost at such a small size.

## Social media links

This is where you add the profiles your site should link to — in the footer or header, depending on how we built your site. For each entry you pick a platform from the list (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, X, YouTube, TikTok) and paste in the full profile address. You can add as many as you need, and remove any that are no longer relevant — say, after closing a profile on a given platform.

## The SEO tab

Site Settings also has its own SEO tab — this is where you set the default title and description that Google shows in search results, for any page that doesn't have its own set. We cover filling in this tab separately: [how do I fill in the SEO tab](./jak-wypelnic-zakladke-seo.md).

Once your name, logo, and social media are in place, it's time for what every visitor sees first — see [how do I edit my homepage](./strona-glowna.md).
