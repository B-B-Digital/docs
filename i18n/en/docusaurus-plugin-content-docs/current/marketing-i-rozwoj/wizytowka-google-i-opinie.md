# Google Business Profile and reviews

Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) is the panel of information about your business that shows up on the right side of Google search results or on Google Maps — with your address, opening hours, photos, and customer reviews. It's a completely different place from your website, but just as important, especially for businesses clients visit in person: a practice, a law firm, or a studio where you run classes.

## Why it matters

When someone searches Google for something like "divorce lawyer Krakow" or "dentist near me," it's your Business Profile that decides whether your business shows up on the map and in the so-called local pack — before anyone even reaches your website. A well-maintained profile, with current hours and replies to reviews, has a real effect on how many calls or messages you get.

## Reviews on your profile vs. reviews on your site

It's easy to mix these up, so it's worth separating them clearly. **Reviews on your Google Business Profile are public reviews collected directly by Google** — visible in search and on Maps, outside your website. That's different from the client testimonials shown directly on your own website (see [How do I add a client testimonial to the site?](../cms/opinia-klienta.md)) — you add those yourself in the CMS panel, and they can come from any source, not just Google.

One doesn't replace the other — it's worth collecting reviews in both places, since they reach different audiences at different stages of searching.
