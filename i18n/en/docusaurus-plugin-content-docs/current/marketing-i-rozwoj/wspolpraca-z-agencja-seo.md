# Working with an SEO or marketing agency

Some of our clients, alongside working with us, also use a separate marketing or SEO agency — a law firm might hire someone to run Google Ads, or a dental practice might work with a consultant on local search rankings. That's a completely normal setup, and we're happy to fit into it.

## Dividing the roles

It's worth agreeing early on who's responsible for what, so nobody ends up doing the same job twice. We build and maintain the website itself: hosting, the domain, the CMS panel, the design, and the technical side of things. A marketing agency usually handles what happens around the site — ad campaigns, SEO, content written for search, and results analysis.

## What a marketing agency usually needs

For a marketing agency to do its job well, it typically needs access to a few things on your end:

- **Google Analytics and Search Console accounts** (see [Google Analytics and Search Console](./google-analytics-i-search-console.md)) — to track how their work is performing
- **your Google Business Profile** — if they're also handling local search
- **specific changes to the site** — like adding a keyword to a service description or building a new page for a campaign

We can grant access to the first two directly to the agency, with no complications — just confirm it with us first.

## How to submit changes requested by your marketing agency

Changes your marketing agency asks for go through us the same way any other request does — you don't need to act as a go-between explaining technical details. You can simply pass along the agency's request, or let us get in touch with them directly, whichever works better for you.

If you're putting together a specific request to pass on, check [how to request a new feature or page](./zgloszenie-nowej-funkcji.md) — it covers what's worth including in a request like that.
