# What is hosting and an SSL certificate?

## Hosting

If a domain is your business's address, then **hosting is the plot of land and the building your business sits in**. It's the place (a server) where your website is physically stored — files, photos, text — and from where it's served to visitors all over the world, 24 hours a day.

Without hosting, a domain would lead nowhere — like an address with no building behind it.

## SSL certificate

An SSL certificate is **the padlock** you see next to a website's address in your browser. It means the connection between a visitor and your site is encrypted and secure — data (e.g. entered into a form) can't be intercepted along the way.

Sites without SSL are flagged by browsers as "not secure," which puts visitors off and hurts a company's credibility. Google also favors sites with SSL in search results.
