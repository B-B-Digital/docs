# What is a CMS and do I need one?

A CMS (content management system) is **a panel where you edit your website's content yourself** — text, photos, prices, opening hours — without needing to know how to code, and without asking a developer for every small change.

## When is it useful?

A CMS makes sense if:

- your offer, prices, or working hours change from time to time
- you want to add news, blog posts, or new projects yourself
- you value being independent from your developer for small updates

If your site is meant to be a simple business card that rarely changes, a simpler site without a CMS works just as well — we'll recommend whichever fits your needs.

## Do I need to know how to code?

No. A CMS panel looks and works much like a text editor or email inbox — you click, type, and save.

## What we take care of

If we go with a CMS, **we'll give you a short training session** on how to use it, and we stay available if anything is unclear.
