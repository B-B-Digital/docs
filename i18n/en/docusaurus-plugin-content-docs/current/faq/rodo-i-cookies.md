# GDPR and cookies — the basics

If you run a practice, a law firm, or a service business in Poland, your website needs to meet a few basic GDPR and cookie requirements. It sounds heavy, but in practice it comes down to a handful of pieces we put together with you.

## What are cookies, and why the banner?

Cookies are small files a site saves in a visitor's browser — to remember their preferences, for instance, or to count a visit in Google Analytics. The consent banner asking about cookies is a legal requirement: a visitor has to be able to accept or decline before anything non-essential starts running.

We configure this banner technically on your behalf. You don't need to write or add any code yourself.

## What's on you

A few things do sit with you as the business owner, not with us as the site's builder:

- your privacy policy should describe your specific business — what data you collect and why (through a contact form, for example)
- if you use extra tools, like a booking system or a newsletter, you decide what customer data you actually need to collect
- you're the data controller for your customers' information — we provide the tool, but your business is legally responsible for how it's used

## Is this enough?

The site we build meets the standard technical requirements — a cookie banner, a baseline privacy policy. If your industry has additional, specific legal requirements — say, you handle sensitive data in a medical practice — it's worth a conversation with a lawyer who knows the details of your business. We're happy to talk through what's technically possible to adjust on the site to match that advice.
